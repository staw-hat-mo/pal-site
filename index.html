Understanding the Israel–Palestine Conflict

An educational research website exploring history, humanitarian impact, and global perspectives.

Historical Background

The Israel–Palestine conflict is a long-standing geopolitical issue shaped by nationalism, territorial claims, and historical events over more than a century.

Key Timeline:

• Late 1800s–1900s: Rise of nationalist movements

• 1917–1948: British Mandate period

• 1948: War and mass displacement (Nakba)

• 1967: Six-Day War and territorial changes

• 2000s–Present: Repeated cycles of conflict and negotiations

Humanitarian Situation

Humanitarian organizations report displacement, infrastructure destruction, and limited access to essential resources such as food, water, and healthcare.

Casualty Statistics

According to UN and humanitarian reports, tens of thousands of Palestinian civilians have been killed since the escalation of the conflict, including large numbers of women and children.

Exact breakdowns vary by source and are continuously updated by organizations such as UN OCHA and humanitarian agencies.

Inspiration

This project was inspired by themes of justice, freedom, and resistance against oppression in the anime One Piece.

The main character, Monkey D. Luffy, is portrayed as someone who challenges oppressive systems and stands up for people who are unable to defend themselves. This idea influenced the way this project focuses on giving attention to humanitarian issues and raising awareness through education.

While this is a fictional story, it reflects broader themes of leadership, empathy, and the importance of supporting those facing injustice in the real world.

In global activism contexts, the phrase “Free Palestine” is often used as a symbolic expression of solidarity.
