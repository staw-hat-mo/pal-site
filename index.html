Understanding the Israel–Palestine Conflict

An educational website exploring the historical background, humanitarian impact, personal testimonies, territorial changes, and global perspectives surrounding the Israel–Palestine conflict.

Historical Background

The Israel–Palestine conflict is a long-standing geopolitical dispute centered around territory, national identity, religion, and political control. Its roots extend back to the late Ottoman Empire and intensified during the British Mandate period after World War I.


In 1947, the United Nations proposed a partition plan dividing the land into Jewish and Arab states. In 1948, the State of Israel was declared, leading to war between Israel and neighboring Arab countries. Hundreds of thousands of Palestinians were displaced during this period, which Palestinians refer to as the Nakba, meaning “catastrophe.”


In 1967, the Six-Day War resulted in Israel taking control of the West Bank, East Jerusalem, and Gaza Strip. Since then, there have been multiple conflicts, peace negotiations, and humanitarian crises affecting civilians on all sides.


Modern discussions about the conflict often focus on human rights, security, displacement, statehood, and humanitarian conditions in Gaza and the West Bank.

Inspiration

This project was partially inspired by themes of justice and freedom presented in the anime One Piece.


The character Monkey D. Luffy often stands against oppressive systems and fights to protect people who are suffering. These themes encouraged the idea of using education, awareness, and research to better understand humanitarian issues.


In many global demonstrations and activist movements, the phrase “Free Palestine” is used as an expression of solidarity with Palestinian civilians and calls for peace, justice, and self-determination.
