Understanding the Israel–Palestine Conflict

An educational research website covering history, humanitarian impact, statistics, and global perspectives.

Historical Background

The Israel–Palestine conflict is a long-standing geopolitical issue rooted in historical, territorial, and national identity claims.

Key historical moments include the end of Ottoman rule, British Mandate (1917–1948), the 1948 Arab–Israeli War (Nakba for Palestinians), and subsequent wars including 1967 and modern Gaza conflicts.

Efforts such as the Oslo Accords attempted peace agreements, but a lasting resolution has not been achieved.

Humanitarian Situation

Humanitarian organizations report severe impacts on civilians, including displacement, infrastructure destruction, and shortages of essential resources such as water, electricity, and medical care.

According to UN reporting, large portions of Gaza’s population have been displaced multiple times during escalations of conflict.

Personal Accounts (Reported by Journalism & NGOs)

Palestinian civilians interviewed by international journalists describe experiences of displacement, loss of homes, and disruption of daily life.

Organizations such as Amnesty International and Human Rights Watch compile verified testimonies from affected families.

These accounts are used to document humanitarian conditions rather than isolated narratives.

Student Inspiration

This project was inspired by themes of justice and freedom seen in popular culture.

This connection is used as a personal motivation, not as a political comparison.
