Understanding the Israel–Palestine Conflict

An educational overview of history, humanitarian impact, personal experiences, and verified global news sources.

History of the Conflict

The Israel–Palestine conflict is a long-standing political and territorial dispute rooted in historical, religious, and national identity claims.

Major events include the end of Ottoman rule, the British Mandate period, the 1948 war (known as the Nakba in Palestinian history), and multiple wars and uprisings since then.

Efforts such as the Oslo Accords attempted peace agreements, but long-term resolution has not been achieved.

Humanitarian Crisis

Humanitarian organizations report ongoing challenges affecting civilians in Gaza and the West Bank.

These include displacement, infrastructure damage, shortages of food, clean water, electricity, and medical supplies.

Aid agencies emphasize that civilian protection and humanitarian access are critical priorities.

Personal Accounts

Palestinian civilians have shared documented testimonies through journalists and humanitarian organizations.

These accounts often describe loss of homes, displacement, and difficulty accessing essential services such as healthcare and education.

Reports from organizations like Human Rights Watch and Amnesty International include verified interviews and field documentation.
